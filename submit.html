Before You Submit

Strong OMEC submissions meet these four criteria. If you're unsure about any of them, start with the Contributor Guidelines.

Built for multilingual learners

Designed from the ground up for ML students — not adapted from a mainstream lesson.

Anchored to a specific text

Every OMEC module centers a single anchor text — a story, article, speech, or other source.

Maintains cognitive rigor

Complex ideas, clear scaffolding. We scaffold access to content — we don't simplify it.

Fits an OMEC strand

S, IT, A, N, or F — or you're not sure. See the strand reference below.

Strand Reference

Select the strand that best matches your anchor text type when filling out the form. Not sure? Choose Other / Not sure and tell us about it.

Strand Name Anchor Text Type
S Short Story A single short story. Analysis-based module structure.
IT Informational Text Nonfiction anchor — articles, speeches, primary sources, legal documents.
A Author / Artist Study Text-centered study of a single author or artist's work and life.
N Novel Extended novel study. Larger scope.

F Comics / Graphic Text Comics, graphic novels, visual text.

? Other / Not sure Doesn't fit a strand, or you're unsure which one applies. Tell us in the form.

What to Expect

All submissions go through review before publication.

  • Confirmation — You'll hear from us within a few days of submitting.
  • Initial review — 2–3 weeks for S, IT, and A strand submissions.
  • Feedback or approval — We'll return notes for revision or move your module to production.
  • Publication — Accepted modules are formatted and published. You'll be credited as the contributing author.

Partial submissions are welcome — you don't need all four documents complete before submitting.
